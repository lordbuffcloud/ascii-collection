# Attribution

ASCII art has a long oral tradition on Usenet, BBSes, and IRC. Many pieces circulate without clear authorship, and many more carry small artist signatures (sigs) embedded in the art itself. This collection tries to preserve those sigs wherever they appear.

## Known sigs to watch for

| Sig | Artist | Status |
|---|---|---|
| `jgs` | Joan G. Stark | Copyrighted. Her estate has historically requested her work not be redistributed. This collection excludes signed `jgs` pieces by default. |
| `hjw` | Hayley Jane Wakenshaw | Copyrighted. Pieces are preserved with sig intact only when quoted in small reference context. |
| `SSt` | Susie Oviatt | Copyrighted. |
| `ejm` | Eric Bowersox / various | Varies. |
| `mrf` | Morfina | Unknown, permissive by convention. |
| `krogg` | Krogg | Community. |

If you see a sig in a piece here, **leave it in place**. Removing it is the one thing the ASCII art community has always agreed is not okay.

## Origin of pieces in this collection

The majority of pieces in this repo fall into these buckets:

1. **Originals** composed for this collection. CC0.
2. **Figlet font output** generated from public-domain figlet fonts (`standard`, `slant`, `big`, `block`, `shadow`, `banner`). Figlet itself is MIT-licensed and its default font catalog is freely redistributable.
3. **Folklore pieces** circulated on Usenet / IRC / BBSes for decades with no known author. Treated as community commons.
4. **Box drawing and Unicode border glyphs** from the public Unicode standard.

## If you are an artist

If you recognize your work in here and want it:

- **Removed** - open an issue titled `[remove] <file path>` and it will be taken down within a day.
- **Credited differently** - open an issue titled `[credit] <file path>` with the preferred credit line.
- **Kept as-is** - no action needed, and thank you.

## Sources consulted

No scraping of live sites was performed to build this collection. Content was composed, regenerated from public-domain tooling (figlet), or drawn from long-circulated folklore pieces that predate and outlive any single host.
